00 — OVERVIEW
Executive Summary

Rently runs one brand across separate domains for each market: rently.sg (Singapore) frames the product around paying, deferring and earning rewards on rent; rently-uae.com (UAE) frames it as Rent Now, Pay Later, converting annual rent into monthly instalments. The two markets have different search behaviour and are reported in their own sections.

The one thing to take away

In both markets Rently is already winning the AI-answer layer: it is mentioned and cited in ~100% of the renter questions we tested across Google AI Overview, Gemini and ChatGPT. In the UAE it is cited more often than Keyper in AI answers despite Keyper having roughly ten times the organic traffic. That visibility is carried almost entirely by blog content. The risk is that the foundation underneath is thin and inconsistent, so the money pages (homepage and product pages) barely get cited, the lead is fragile, and competitors can catch up. Fixing the foundation is how Rently converts AI visibility into durable rankings, sign-ups, and a defensible position.

Same pattern, bigger opportunity

Rently UAE is cited 70 times vs Keyper's 21 in these AI answers, again almost entirely through blog pages. Yet in traditional organic search Keyper is far ahead (~4,000 monthly visits vs Rently's ~435). Rently is already ahead where search is going (AI answers) and behind where it is today (blue links). Fixing the sitemap, schema and blog maintenance protects the AI lead and starts closing the organic gap.

03 — ACTION
What to fix first

Ordered by impact-to-effort. Foundation fixes protect the AI lead and route citations toward the pages that convert.

Singapore · rently.sg

  • Make the homepage H1 visible and keyword-aligned (stop relying on the hidden sr-only heading).
  • Fix duplicate-brand titles sitewide; give the homepage a transactional focus keyword ("pay rent online / by card").
  • Add real JSON-LD for the rich types: FAQPage (convert the existing microdata), BreadcrumbList and Article/BlogPosting; fix the invalid blog block.
  • Add breadcrumbs to blog pages (Home > Blog > Category > Article).
  • Audit the ~120 non-performing blog pages (intent / cannibalisation) and consolidate.
  • Fix mobile speed (22.5s LCP) on the homepage.

UAE · rently-uae.com

  • Add blog posts to the XML sitemap and submit in Search Console (fastest win).
  • Standardise the product-page JSON-LD across all templates: add Organization + WebSite sitewide, and fix the homepage (0 schema) and the broken blog JSON-LD.
  • Rewrite the homepage title around the real product ("Rent Now, Pay Later / pay rent monthly in the UAE").
  • Reactivate the blog against high-intent clusters (RERA rent calculator, pay rent by card, split annual rent).
  • Add breadcrumbs to blog pages; normalise the canonical vs sitemap trailing slash.
  • Improve mobile speed (7.5s LCP).
Why this matters now

Rently's AI-answer lead in both markets is real but fragile because it rests on blog content over a thin, inconsistent foundation. Competitors (CardUp in Singapore, Keyper in the UAE) are one content-and-schema push away from contesting it. The foundation work in this document is what turns a fragile lead into a durable one, and points AI citations at the pages that actually convert renters into sign-ups.
