00 — OVERVIEW
Executive Summary

Rently runs one brand across separate domains for each market: rently.sg (Singapore) frames the product around paying, deferring and earning rewards on rent; rently-uae.com (UAE) frames it as Rent Now, Pay Later, converting annual rent into monthly instalments. The two markets have different search behaviour and must be treated separately, which is why this analysis reports each domain in its own section.

The one thing to take away

In both markets Rently is already winning the AI-answer layer: it is mentioned and cited in ~100% of the renter questions we tested across Google AI Overview, Gemini and ChatGPT. In the UAE it is cited more often than Keyper in AI answers despite Keyper having roughly ten times the organic traffic. That visibility is carried almost entirely by blog content. The risk is that the foundation underneath is thin, so the money pages (homepage and product pages) barely get cited, the lead is fragile, and competitors can catch up. Fixing the foundation is how Rently converts AI visibility into durable rankings, sign-ups, and a defensible position.

00 — METHOD
How we validated every finding

Nothing here is assumed. Each finding was checked against the live sites before it was written up. Where the raw evidence contradicted an initial read, we corrected it, two items were reframed after inspection.

  • Live server HTML was fetched for both homepages, product pages and sample blog pages, and inspected for titles, headings, schema and breadcrumbs.
  • Structured data was extracted directly from the page source (JSON-LD and inline microdata), not estimated.
  • Sitemaps were downloaded and URL counts measured (total vs blog).
  • Core Web Vitals were measured on mobile via Lighthouse.
  • AI visibility was measured by running 15 real renter questions per market through Google AI Overview, Gemini and ChatGPT, and recording whether Rently was mentioned and cited, and which pages were cited.
Two of the initial findings were corrected after inspection

REFRAMED  SG "old-design cache" → the server HTML is actually current. The real cause is that the H1 and most section headings are marked class="sr-only" (deliberately hidden from sighted users), so the heading Google and AI read differs from the visible hero. It is a heading-visibility issue, not a caching bug.

REFRAMED  "No structured data" → SG does have some schema (WebSite + WebPage + FinancialServices); UAE has FAQ Question/Answer microdata. Both are incomplete rather than absent. The recommendation still stands, it just needs to be framed as "complete the schema", not "add schema from zero".

The catch: the blog is doing all the work

Of the 35 distinct Rently URLs cited across the run, 28 were blog pages and only 7 were the homepage or product pages. AI is recommending Rently through articles, not through the pages that actually convert a renter into a sign-up. Strengthening the homepage and product-page titles, schema and headings is how those citations start pointing at the money pages.

Same pattern, bigger opportunity

Rently UAE is cited 70 times vs Keyper's 21 in these AI answers, again almost entirely through blog pages. Yet in traditional organic search Keyper is far ahead (~4,000 monthly visits vs Rently's ~435). Rently is already ahead where search is going (AI answers) and behind where it is today (blue links). Fixing the sitemap, schema and blog maintenance protects the AI lead and starts closing the organic gap.

03 — ACTION
What to fix first

Ordered by impact-to-effort. Foundation fixes protect the AI lead and route citations toward the pages that convert.

Singapore · rently.sg

  • Make the homepage H1 visible and keyword-aligned (stop relying on the hidden sr-only heading).
  • Fix duplicate-brand titles sitewide; give the homepage a transactional focus keyword ("pay rent online / by card").
  • Complete the schema: add FAQPage, BreadcrumbList and Article/BlogPosting.
  • Add breadcrumbs to blog pages (Home > Blog > Category > Article).
  • Audit the ~120 non-performing blog pages (intent / cannibalisation) and consolidate.
  • Fix mobile speed (22.5s LCP) on the homepage.

UAE · rently-uae.com

  • Add blog posts to the XML sitemap and submit in Search Console (fastest win).
  • Implement JSON-LD from the ground up: Organization, WebSite, Product/Service, FAQPage, BreadcrumbList.
  • Rewrite the homepage title around the real product ("Rent Now, Pay Later / pay rent monthly in the UAE").
  • Reactivate the blog against high-intent clusters (RERA rent calculator, pay rent by card, split annual rent).
  • Add breadcrumbs to blog pages; normalise the canonical vs sitemap trailing slash.
  • Improve mobile speed (7.5s LCP).
Why this matters now

Rently's AI-answer lead in both markets is real but fragile because it rests on blog content over a thin foundation. Competitors (CardUp in Singapore, Keyper in the UAE) are one content-and-schema push away from contesting it. The foundation work in this document is what turns a fragile lead into a durable, defensible one, and points AI citations at the pages that actually convert renters into sign-ups.
