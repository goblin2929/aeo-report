Executive Summary

Month in Review

February 2026 delivered record-breaking growth for GoFreight's organic search visibility. Total clicks surged to 4,424 (+33.8% MoM), while impressions nearly tripled to 1,923,668 (+157.0%). Average position improved dramatically from 15.3 to 8.4, reflecting stronger SERP placements across the board. Key drivers:

  • Blog explosion: Blog clicks up +124.9% (1,128 to 2,537), accounting for the vast majority of site growth
  • Non-branded dominance: Non-branded clicks grew +60.0%, driving 113.4% of net click growth (offsetting brand decline)
  • Core keyword wins: Top-10 keyword coverage jumped from 3 to 6 keywords (50% coverage), including new entries for "freight management software" and "freight management system software"
  • Supply chain optimization article: Single blog post generated 691,853 impressions, demonstrating massive topical reach

AEO Performance: Major Breakthrough in February

AI visibility metrics saw a dramatic improvement in February, validating the SEO and content strategy investments. Key observations:

  • Visibility surged from 14.3% to 23.5% (+9.2pp): Largest single month gain since tracking began
  • Mentions exploded from 29 to 403: Driven by increased LLM citations of GoFreight blog content
  • Avg Position improved from 2.0 to 1.7 (-0.3): GoFreight consistently cited near the top of AI responses
  • AI traffic up 49% (162 sessions): First key events (2) from AI referrer traffic, indicating conversion potential
  • Comparison content working: "CargoWise Pricing" and "CargoWise vs GoFreight" are top AI landing pages

Gap to close: GoFreight ranks #4 in visibility (23.5%) behind CargoWise (34.0%), Magaya (32.0%), and Descartes (29.7%). The gap is narrowing fast. Continued content velocity and schema deployment should push GoFreight into the top 3 by Q2.
