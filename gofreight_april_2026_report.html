Executive Summary

Month in Review — April 2026

April was a heavy publishing month — 25 pieces shipped, weighted toward glossary builds (16 of 25) on April 20–22. Traffic took a sharp step down vs March: clicks fell 30.2% and impressions fell 40.1%, with average position drifting from 7.74 to 9.72. The decline is concentrated in Blog (the volume engine that carried Q1) — Blog clicks dropped from 2,890 to 1,476, mirroring the loss in non-branded discovery traffic. Glossary clicks grew +173% (76 → 208) as the freshly-published cluster started to land — a leading indicator that the April content investment will compound in May/June. Branded traffic held flat (~1,170 clicks vs ~1,280) while non-branded discovery (the Blog engine) lost the most ground.

KPI Outcome Analysis

Click Volume — Below Target

Clicks dropped 30.2% MoM (5,067 → 3,535). The decline is concentrated in Blog subfolder (-49%), reflecting search-volume drop on freight-software queries (yearly trend on core terms is -50% to -86% — see Section 10) plus end-of-month dip from algorithm fluctuation in late April.

Impression Volume — Below Target

Impressions fell 40.1%. Steepest decline came from cached high-traffic blog URLs that carried inherited authority pre-Q1 cleanup. The new April content is not yet ranked at scale (most pages 0–14 days old).

CTR — Improving

Despite traffic decline, CTR improved to 0.34% (+16% relative). Impressions fell faster than clicks — meaning the queries we DID surface for were higher-intent. This is consistent with ranking the new commercial-intent glossary pages.

Average Position — Worsening

Position drifted from 7.74 to 9.72 (+25.5%). Expected during a publishing-heavy month — newly-indexed pages enter at lower ranks and pull the impression-weighted average up. Should self-correct as April pages mature (4–8 weeks).

⚠ Critical Issues

  • Blog subfolder bleed: -49% clicks, -43% impressions. Need to identify the top 5 declining blog URLs and run content-refresh diagnostics. Likely candidates: pages from the pre-Q1 archive that lost rankings to fresher competitor content.
  • Product Attribute Pages stuck: 11 Product Attribute Pages delivered to GoFreight but only 1 of 11 (Customer Portal) went live in April. These are the highest commercial-intent pages in the pipeline. Recommend sync with GoFreight product team to clear bottleneck.
  • Workduo data gap: AEO visibility tracking only captured 4 of 30 days (Apr 5–6, Apr 28–29). Workduo project ingestion appears intermittent — needs operational fix before May report or AEO trend will be uninterpretable.

Next Month Priorities (May 2026)

  • Unblock Product Attribute Pages — push GoFreight to publish the 10 remaining /product/* pages. These target highest-intent commercial keywords.
  • Blog refresh sprint — identify and refresh the 10 declining Blog URLs that drove March traffic but lost ground in April.
  • Fix Workduo daily ingestion — verify the API job is running daily; without it AEO competitor visibility cannot be trended.
  • Track April content lift — May should show the 16 new glossary pages start ranking. Measure new-page contribution explicitly.
  • Internal linking for new glossary cluster (16 pages published Apr 20–22) — most are still orphaned; linking them from existing high-authority pages will accelerate ranking.

Core Keyword Insights

  • Strong commercial-intent rankings: "freight management system software" jumped to #2 (from #4); "air freight forwarding software" jumped to #2 (from #4). Both URLs (homepage + best-fms blog) capturing AI Overview placements.
  • New entrants: "freight management system" (vol 320, #7 NEW) and "global freight management system" (vol 30, #10 NEW) — the best-fms guide is starting to rank for the broader category, not just the modifier-heavy variants.
  • Gaps: "ocean freight management software" still page 4 (#41) — homepage isn't an ideal target for ocean-specific intent. The forthcoming /solutions/ocean hub page (delivered, awaiting GoFreight publish) is the right answer.
  • Industry headwind: Search volume on every core term is down YoY (-19% to -86%). This is category-wide, not GoFreight-specific. Position improvement is the right defense — capture more of a shrinking pie.
