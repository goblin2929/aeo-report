// 01 · Executive Summary

Strong at attracting readers, weak at reaching the product

OCBC Indonesia already wins a very large audience through educational content. The Ruang meNYALA articles bring in the overwhelming majority of the site's organic visitors. The problem is that this traffic is not turning into product visibility.

The pages that actually sell tabungan, deposito, KPR, KTA, and the other core products attract only a small fraction of visitors, and they are built in a way that makes them hard for both Google and AI platforms to understand. In plain terms, OCBC is excellent at getting people to read, and weak at getting people to the product.

Why this happens
Diagnosis

About 95 percent of OCBC's organic traffic comes from the blog and articles. Only a small share reaches the product pages. The educational engine is strong, but the bridge from that audience into product pages is weak, and the product pages themselves are not built to rank or to be cited by AI. The findings below explain exactly why.

// 03 · Technical Findings

How the site speaks to search engines and AI

3.1  Structured data not maximised on homepage and product pages
TechnicalHigh Impact
What we found

Structured data coverage is minimal. Our crawl confirmed that the homepage and product pages carry only a Breadcrumb markup, with no Organization, no Website, and no product or service markup.

Why it matters

Structured data is how a site explains itself to machines. Beyond the few types Google shows visually, there is a wider library of universal schema.org types that AI platforms use to understand entities, products, and providers. Right now OCBC gives AI platforms very little to work with, which weakens how OCBC appears in AI generated answers.

Recommendation

Build a full structured data layer. Add Organization and Website markup sitewide, and product or financial service markup to each product page. Use the broader schema.org vocabulary, not only the types Google renders.

3.2  No FAQ structured data
TechnicalMedium Impact
What we found

Pages that already display an FAQ do not include FAQ structured data.

  • https://www.ocbc.id/id/individu/simpanan/tabungan/tanda360-komunitas
Why it matters

Structured data is a machine readable label that tells Google and AI "this is a question and this is its answer." When present, Google can show questions directly in search results and AI tools can quote the answers confidently. The FAQ content is already written. It is simply not labelled, so the value is left on the table.

Recommendation

Add FAQPage structured data to every page that has an FAQ section.

3.3  Calculator links point to empty pages
TechnicalMedium Impact
What we found

Calculator entries inside the navigation bar point to null or non existent pages.

Why it matters

Customers deciding on a tabungan berjangka or a deposito want to estimate returns before committing. A broken calculator link is a dead end at exactly the moment a customer is most interested, and it wastes the search opportunity around simulation keywords.

Recommendation

Build dedicated, working calculator pages (see Future Development).

3.4  No llms.txt file
TechnicalAI Readiness
What we found

The site has no llms.txt file.

Why it matters

llms.txt is an emerging standard that gives AI platforms a clean map of a site's structure and key products, similar to how a sitemap helps search engines. Without it, AI tools piece together OCBC's product structure on their own, which they often do imperfectly.

Recommendation

Publish an llms.txt that describes the main path structure and the core product lines so AI platforms get accurate context about OCBC.

3.5  No x-default hreflang tag
TechnicalQuick Win
What we found

The site does not declare an x-default version in its hreflang setup.

Why it matters

hreflang tells search engines which language version to show to which user. The x-default is the fallback for users whose language is not explicitly covered. Without it, Google has to guess which version to serve, which can lead to the wrong page appearing in results.

Recommendation

Add an x-default hreflang tag pointing to the Indonesian (id) version as the default.

// 04 · Content Findings

How clearly each page explains its product

4.1  No descriptive content for similar products
ContentHigh Impact
What we found

Similar products lack clear on page descriptions, and key information is locked inside PDF files.

  • https://www.ocbc.id/id/individu/simpanan/tabungan/tabunganku
  • https://www.ocbc.id/id/individu/simpanan/tabungan/tanda360-komunitas
Why it matters

When product details sit in a PDF, search engines and AI treat that as a separate file outside the page, so the page itself looks thin and uninformative. Customers also have to download a document just to understand a product. Thin pages do not rank and do not get cited.

Recommendation

Bring the key product information onto the page as readable text, so customers, crawlers, and AI can all understand each product directly. Strengthening the FAQ section is a fast way to add this context.

4.2  No Heading 1 on core business pages
ContentHigh Impact
What we found

Core product pages have no Heading 1.

  • https://www.ocbc.id/id/individu/simpanan/tabungan-berjangka
Why it matters

The Heading 1 is the single most important on page signal of what a page is about. A page with no Heading 1 is like a document with no title. Our crawl confirmed this pattern across the core pages.

Recommendation

Give every core business page a single clear Heading 1 that states the product and its main benefit.

4.3  Page titles of core pages need rewriting
ContentMedium Impact
What we found

The browser titles of several core pages are not built around clear target keywords.

  • https://www.ocbc.id/id/individu/pinjaman/kredit-pemilikan-rumah
  • https://www.ocbc.id/id/individu/pinjaman/kredit-pembelian-mobil/kpm-ocbc
Why it matters

The title tag is what shows as the clickable line in search results. A vague title earns fewer clicks and ranks for fewer relevant searches. A clear, benefit led title does both.

Recommendation

Rewrite the titles around the products and their benefits. Suggested direction:

  • KPR page: "Kredit Pemilikan Rumah (KPR): Wujudkan Rumah Impian Anda Lebih Mudah, Angsuran Rendah dan Hingga 25 Tahun"
  • KPM page: "Kredit Pemilikan Mobil: Beli Mobil Impian Baru atau Second Lebih Mudah, Suku Bunga Ringan hingga 5 Tahun"
4.4  Duplicate page titles across products
ContentMedium Impact
What we found

Different products share the same visible title (currently set as Heading 2).

  • https://www.ocbc.id/id/individu/simpanan/tabungan-berjangka
  • https://www.ocbc.id/id/individu/simpanan/deposito
Why it matters

When two different products use the same title, search engines and AI cannot tell them apart, and they may compete against each other for the same searches.

Recommendation

Make the main title (which should be the Heading 1) unique for every product within each business group.

// 05 · Future Development

New capabilities to build

5.1  Build calculator and simulation pages
DevelopmentHigh Impact
What we found

There are no working calculator pages for KPR, deposito, kredit mobil, and similar products.

Why it matters

Indonesians actively search "simulasi" and "kalkulator" terms before choosing a bank. These are high intent searches from people close to a decision. Without these pages, OCBC is invisible for that demand, and competitors who built simulators capture it.

Recommendation

Create dedicated calculator pages for deposito, KPR, and kredit mobil. They serve customers at the decision moment and open a valuable set of simulation keywords for both Search and AI platforms.

Traffic opportunity (Ahrefs, Indonesia)

These pages target high intent keywords worth roughly 28,000 generic searches a month. Modelled capture: about +2,600 extra organic clicks per month within the first 6 to 12 months (range 1,700 to 3,700), growing to about +8,900 per month at maturity (range 6,200 to 11,700). At maturity that would lift OCBC's product page traffic by roughly half from this one initiative.

5.2  Add an SEO content section to category and parent pages
DevelopmentMedium Impact
What we found

Category and parent pages have no space for descriptive content.

  • https://www.ocbc.id/id/syariah/pembiayaan
  • https://www.ocbc.id/id/syariah/pembiayaan/kpr
  • https://www.ocbc.id/id/individu/pinjaman/kredit-pemilikan-rumah
Why it matters

Parent and category pages are often the ones that rank for broad, high value searches. With no room for content, the SEO team cannot give these pages the context they need to compete, so they underperform.

Recommendation

Add a content section at the bottom of category and parent pages so the team can provide supporting context for Search and AI platforms.

5.3  Add breadcrumb navigation
DevelopmentQuick Win
What we found

Some pages lack breadcrumb navigation.

  • https://www.ocbc.id/id/sme/kelola-dana-bisnis/simpanan/tanda360-plus
Why it matters

Breadcrumbs show users and crawlers where a page sits in the site. They help visitors navigate, help search engines understand the structure, and can appear directly in search results.

Recommendation

Add breadcrumbs, for example: Home > SME > Kelola Dana Bisnis > Simpanan > Tanda 360 Plus.

// 06 · What to Prioritise

Sequencing the work by commercial impact

The data and the findings point to one clear theme: the product pages are the weak link. We recommend tackling the highest impact work first.

  1. Fix the product pages so they can rank and be understood. Add a unique Heading 1, rewrite the titles, and bring product details out of PDFs onto the page (4.1, 4.2, 4.3, 4.4). This directly addresses the 69 visitors per page problem.
  2. Add the structured data layer. Organization and Website sitewide, product or financial service markup on product pages, and FAQ markup wherever an FAQ exists (3.1, 3.2). This is largely labelling work on content that already exists.
  3. Build the calculator and simulation pages to capture high intent decision searches (5.1). Modelled at about +2,600 extra organic clicks per month within a year, growing to about +8,900 at maturity. Start with deposito.
  4. Strengthen technical foundations for AI: llms.txt, x-default hreflang, and breadcrumbs (3.4, 3.5, 5.3).
  5. Open up category and parent pages with a content section so the SEO team can compete for broad searches (5.2).
