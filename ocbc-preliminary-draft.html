// 01 · Executive Summary

Strong at attracting readers, weak at reaching the product

OCBC Indonesia already wins a very large audience through educational content. The Ruang meNYALA articles bring in the overwhelming majority of the site's organic visitors. The problem is that this traffic is not turning into product visibility.

The pages that actually sell tabungan, deposito, KPR, KTA, and the other core products attract only a small fraction of visitors, and they are built in a way that makes them hard for both Google and AI platforms to understand. In plain terms, OCBC is excellent at getting people to read, and weak at getting people to the product.

How to read this

The total year 1 ceiling is about plus 44,000 visits a month, which is about 528,000 additional visits across the first year. It is led by the blog: OCBC's blog already earns about 1,190,000 visits a month, and the plus 39,000 blog share here is what it can win in year 1 by covering financial topics it is currently missing, about 3 percent on top of what it has today. The calculator and simulation pages recommended in the Future Development section are a separate, additional opportunity (about plus 2,600 visits a month) that is not included in this table, so the two can be added together. The smartest move is to point the blog traffic at the two priority products, by writing topics like "tabungan tanpa biaya admin", "kartu kredit cicilan 0%", and "cara buka rekening online" instead of chasing visits that never become customers. This is a directional estimate because OCBC Search Console is not yet connected, so the ranges tighten once it is.

How these numbers are calculated
Methodology

Step 1: Addressable non branded demand. From Ahrefs we take every keyword competitors rank for but OCBC does not, then strip out branded terms (bri, mandiri, bca, cimb and other bank names), noise (kurs, calendars, horoscopes, phone credit), and non financial topics. The remaining keywords, summed by monthly search volume, are the demand OCBC could realistically chase: about 2,400,000 financial searches a month.

Step 2: From searches to visits, through two simple filters. Search volume is total demand, but not every search becomes a visit. Two things shrink it:

  • Clickable factor (40 percent) is how many searches still produce a click. Many searches never reach any website, because the answer already sits on the results page: paid ads (SEM) at the top, plus AI Overview, Knowledge Panel, and featured snippets. We assume only about 40 of every 100 searches still lead to an organic click. So 2,400,000 searches become about 975,000 clickable visits.
  • Capture rate (about 4 percent in year 1) is OCBC's share of those clicks. The clicks are split across every site that ranks, not just OCBC. Competing with established players but backed by its own blog authority (about 1,190,000 visits a month), OCBC can realistically win about 4 of every 100 clickable visits in year 1. So 975,000 becomes about 39,000 visits a month.
The flow in one line

2,400,000 searches, minus ads, AI Overview and Knowledge Panel (40 percent clickable), equals about 975,000 clickable visits, times OCBC's winnable share (about 4 percent), equals about plus 39,000 visits a month. Savings and Credit Card use a different basis (the gap has almost no non branded product keywords), so they are benchmarked against what competitors already earn on those product pages. Directional estimate; the 40 percent and 4 percent are conservative and tighten once OCBC Search Console is connected.

This analysis groups the findings into three areas: Technical, Content, and Future Development. Each finding explains what we found, why it matters for a non technical reader, and what we recommend.

// 04 · Technical Findings

How the site speaks to search engines and AI

4.1  Structured data not maximised on homepage and product pages
TechnicalHigh Impact
What we found

Structured data coverage is minimal. Our crawl confirmed that the homepage and product pages carry only a Breadcrumb markup, with no Organization, no Website, and no product or service markup.

Why it matters

Structured data is how a site explains itself to machines. Beyond the few types Google shows visually, there is a wider library of universal schema.org types that AI platforms use to understand entities, products, and providers. Right now OCBC gives AI platforms very little to work with, which weakens how OCBC appears in AI generated answers.

Recommendation

Build a full structured data layer. Add Organization and Website markup sitewide, and product or financial service markup to each product page. Use the broader schema.org vocabulary, not only the types Google renders.

4.2  No FAQ structured data
TechnicalMedium Impact
What we found

Pages that already display an FAQ do not include FAQ structured data.

  • https://www.ocbc.id/id/individu/simpanan/tabungan/tanda360-komunitas
Why it matters

Structured data is a machine readable label that tells Google and AI "this is a question and this is its answer." When present, Google can show questions directly in search results and AI tools can quote the answers confidently. The FAQ content is already written. It is simply not labelled, so the value is left on the table.

Recommendation

Add FAQPage structured data to every page that has an FAQ section.

4.3  Calculator links point to empty pages
TechnicalMedium Impact
What we found

Calculator entries inside the navigation bar point to null or non existent pages.

Why it matters

Customers deciding on a tabungan berjangka or a deposito want to estimate returns before committing. A broken calculator link is a dead end at exactly the moment a customer is most interested, and it wastes the search opportunity around simulation keywords.

Recommendation

Build dedicated, working calculator pages (see Future Development).

4.4  No llms.txt file
TechnicalAI Readiness
What we found

The site has no llms.txt file.

Why it matters

llms.txt is an emerging standard that gives AI platforms a clean map of a site's structure and key products, similar to how a sitemap helps search engines. Without it, AI tools piece together OCBC's product structure on their own, which they often do imperfectly.

Recommendation

Publish an llms.txt that describes the main path structure and the core product lines so AI platforms get accurate context about OCBC.

4.5  No x-default hreflang tag
TechnicalQuick Win
What we found

The site does not declare an x-default version in its hreflang setup.

Why it matters

hreflang tells search engines which language version to show to which user. The x-default is the fallback for users whose language is not explicitly covered. Without it, Google has to guess which version to serve, which can lead to the wrong page appearing in results.

Recommendation

Add an x-default hreflang tag pointing to the Indonesian (id) version as the default.

6.4  Restructure blog content so search engines and AI can understand and cite it
ContentHigh Impact
What we found

The blog is OCBC's biggest traffic asset, but most articles are written as long prose. Search engines and AI platforms extract and cite content far more easily when it is structured into clear, answerable blocks. The current structure makes it harder for AI to pull a clean answer from an OCBC article and attribute it to OCBC.

Why it matters

AI answers and Google featured snippets lift short, self contained answers, definitions, lists, and question and answer blocks. A wall of text is harder to extract and less likely to be cited. Better structure increases the chance that OCBC's blog, and the product content inside it, gets quoted in AI answers and rich results. It is also how OCBC turns its existing blog audience into product visibility.

Recommendation

Adopt one content structure on every article going forward:

  • One clear Heading 1 stating the topic, then a logical Heading 2 and Heading 3 hierarchy with descriptive, question style subheadings.
  • A short answer or key takeaways box at the very top (two to four sentences) that directly answers the main question, so AI can lift it.
  • Concise, self contained definitions and direct answers near the top of each section, not buried in long paragraphs.
  • A FAQ section at the bottom with real questions and short answers, marked up with FAQPage structured data.
  • Lists, numbered steps, and comparison tables for anything procedural or comparative, because these are extracted directly into AI answers and snippets.
  • Article structured data (headline, author, published and updated dates) plus a visible last updated date for freshness.
  • Natural internal links from the article to the relevant product page, for example a savings article links to the savings product page, so blog authority flows toward Savings and Credit Card.

DevelopmentHigh Impact
What we found

That structure lets BCA rank not just for "promo kartu kredit" but for many specific category queries like "promo kartu kredit e-commerce".

Why it matters

A single promo page can target only one broad keyword. Breaking promos into categories (e-commerce, dining, travel, groceries, and so on) creates a dedicated, indexable page for each high intent query a cardholder actually searches, so OCBC can appear for many more promo searches instead of one. Beyond traffic, these pages also raise awareness of the benefits OCBC cardholders already have, which supports card usage and retention.

Recommendation

Build a promo hub with category pages, the way BCA does. Keep the main /promo page, then add crawlable category pages underneath it (for example /promo/e-commerce, /promo/dining, /promo/travel, /promo/groceries), each listing the relevant active promos as clear, indexable text. Refresh them regularly so they stay current and keep ranking. This also directly fixes the promo message gap noted earlier, where AI recommends competitors for "promo kartu kredit" because they have a dedicated, crawlable promo structure and OCBC does not.

Traffic opportunity

The promo and credit card category group represents about 1,900 searches a month that OCBC's single promo page cannot capture today. The value is not only that traffic. A structured promo hub also strengthens awareness of the benefits OCBC cardholders already hold, supporting usage and retention beyond the direct visits.

The data and the findings point to one clear theme: the product pages are the weak link. We recommend tackling the highest impact work first.
