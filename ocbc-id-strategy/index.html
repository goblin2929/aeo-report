// 01 EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Largest Reach in Indonesian Banking — and the Lowest Commercial Yield

OCBC doesn't have a traffic problem — it has a traffic-relevance problem and an AI-visibility gap. It has built the largest banking content footprint in Indonesia (3,664 indexed pages, 1.25M organic visits a month) and the lowest yield per product page in its competitive set: 69 visits, versus 347 to 1,457 for peers. Only 18.6% of that blog traffic carries commercial intent, and the product pages that sell capture almost none of it. The same gap shows up in AI: OCBC is cited in just 60% of the non-branded questions real buyers ask — and usually through a blog article, not a product page.

The opportunity is not more traffic; it's converting earned reach into product demand, and closing that 60% so OCBC becomes the answer AI gives at the moment of decision. Expect moderate growth in total traffic — by design — and outsized growth in buyer-relevant traffic and product-level AI citations. It is not about producing more content; it's about rebalancing what gets written, and how it's written.

Intent rebalance

// 04 AI VISIBILITY & THE BUYER'S MOMENT

#2 for Brand Awareness in AI — #4 at the Buyer's Moment

OCBC's AI presence looks strong on the surface: it is #2 for brand awareness in AI — how often AI mentions OCBC across broad banking topics (31.4% share of voice, ahead of Mandiri and BRI). But brand awareness is not the same as being cited at the buyer's moment.

The buyer's moment is when someone is choosing a product and asks a specific question in their own words — the commercial- and transactional-intent end of the funnel (MOFU + BOFU), not general browsing.

// 05 TECHNICAL & CONTENT FINDINGS

What's Holding the Money Pages Back

The implementation detail behind Gap 2 — what we found, why it matters, and the fix. Most of this is labelling and on-page work on pages that already exist.

Content findings

No Heading 1 on core product pages

The H1 is the strongest signal of what a page is about, for both search engines and AI. Core product pages (e.g. tabungan berjangka) have none. Fix: give every product page a single, clear, intent-matched H1 stating the product and its main benefit.

Duplicate page titles across products (keyword cannibalization)

Different products share the same visible title — e.g. tabungan berjangka and deposito — so search engines and AI can't tell them apart and they compete for the same searches. Fix: a unique title (and H1) for every product.

Titles not built around target keywords

Core page titles (KPR, KPM) are not built around the terms buyers search. Fix: rewrite around the product + benefit, e.g. "Kredit Pemilikan Rumah (KPR): Angsuran Rendah hingga 25 Tahun."

Thin product pages — detail trapped in PDFs

Key product information sits inside PDF files, so the page itself looks thin and uninformative to crawlers and AI, and customers must download a document to understand a product. Fix: bring the key information onto the page as readable text; strengthen the on-page FAQ.

Technical findings

Structured data not maximised

The homepage and product pages carry only Breadcrumb markup — no Organization, Website, or Product/financial-service schema. AI platforms have very little to work with. Fix: a full structured-data layer (Organization + Website sitewide; Product/FinancialProduct on product pages; FAQPage wherever an FAQ exists).

No FAQ structured data on pages that already have FAQs

FAQ content is written on the page but not labelled, so Google and AI can't lift it confidently. Fix: add FAQPage structured data to every page with an FAQ.

No llms.txt file

llms.txt gives AI platforms a clean map of the site's structure and core products. Without it, AI pieces OCBC's product structure together imperfectly. Fix: publish an llms.txt describing the main path structure and core product lines.

No x-default hreflang · broken calculator links · missing breadcrumbs

No x-default hreflang (Google must guess which version to serve unmatched visitors); calculator links in the navigation point to empty pages (a dead end at peak interest); some pages lack breadcrumb navigation. Fix: add x-default hreflang to the Indonesian version; build working calculator pages (Section 06); add breadcrumbs sitewide.

// 06 THE PLAN

Two Engines Convert Earned Reach Into Qualified Leads

The plan runs two engines in parallel: one captures the demand OCBC already ranks for, the other wins the buyer at the AI layer.

Engine 1

Technical & on-page SEO on the money pages

Capture demand OCBC already has.

  • ▸Unique, intent-matched H1 and title tag on every core product page
  • ▸Product detail out of PDFs and onto the page as crawlable text
  • ▸Internal links from relevant blog into matching product pages (route link equity)
  • ▸Structured data: Organization, Website, Product/FinancialProduct, FAQPage, Breadcrumb

Why first: highest certainty, fastest payback — optimization of pages that already exist and already have authority behind them.

Engine 2

Relevance Engineering / AEO

Win the buyer inside AI answers.

  • ▸Make product pages retrievable and citable — reverse the 77% blog / 23% product split
  • ▸Publish the missing message in buyer framing — close the message gap
  • ▸Commercial-intent assets: calculators, a structured promo hub, category / comparison pages
  • ▸llms.txt + snippet-extractable formatting (key-takeaways, FAQ schema, tables)

Relevance Engineering: optimizing content so an AI model retrieves, trusts and cites it — the AI-era complement to ranking.

The rebalance, not "more": prune or consolidate the off-topic informational content and reinvest that budget into commercial-intent topics and money-page depth. It is not about producing more content — it's about rebalancing what gets written, and how it's written.
