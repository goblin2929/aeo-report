AIA has the brand strength and the website authority to win far more non branded search traffic than it does today. The gap is not authority. It is how the site is built for crawlers, how product pages speak to buyers, and how little educational content AIA publishes against the opportunity.

// 01 · Executive Summary

Strong brand, under used authority

AIA Singapore is the second most visible insurer in organic search in this group, with around 82,346 organic visits a month across 554 ranking pages. It is a brand that people actively search for, and AI assistants already rate it highly.

The problem sits underneath that strength. About 71 percent of AIA traffic comes from people searching the brand name. Non branded discovery, where new customers find an insurer without naming one, is where AIA falls behind. Great Eastern earns roughly double AIA's non branded traffic and more than six times its non branded product traffic, despite having website authority that is only a few points higher. AIA is sitting on the authority to compete and is not yet using it, because the product pages are hard for crawlers to reach and understand, and because AIA publishes very little educational content where most new buyers begin.

The one line that matters

AI knows AIA well, but cites the blog, not the products
AI AnswersStrength

We tested how AIA appears in live AI answers across Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, and Perplexity. The visibility is genuinely strong. Perplexity called AIA "the best overall life insurance company in Singapore," and ChatGPT listed AIA first among Singapore insurers. AI already knows AIA and rates it highly.

The catch is what gets cited as the source. Across every non branded question we tested, when AI pointed to an AIA owned page, it pointed to the same single educational guide:

  • /en/health-wellness/healthy-living-with-aia/5-types-of-insurance-you-should-get-in-singapore
The one line that matters

The more valuable category and comparison citations went to third party portals like MoneySmart, SingSaver, and PlannerBee instead of to AIA. AI talks about AIA using other people's pages. Fixing the technical foundation and building category content turns AIA from a brand that AI mentions into a brand that AI cites.

Where AIA has a clear, crawlable product page, it wins. On travel insurance for a visa AIA is absent entirely despite selling a capable plan.

// 03 · Technical Findings

Why the pages AIA already has are hard to find and trust

Based on a Screaming Frog crawl of 713 URLs run on 29 June 2026, a homepage Lighthouse test, and live page checks. Each finding lists what we found, why it matters, and the fix.

3.1 · Product links inside hub pages cannot be crawled
TechnicalHigh impact
What we found

On hub pages such as the accident protection hub, the links to individual product detail pages are not delivered in a way crawlers can follow, because they depend on the browser to build them after the page loads.

  • https://www.aia.com.sg/en/our-products/accident-protection
Visual evidence

The same Health category page, seen two ways. On the left is what a person sees in the browser: rich product cards for AIA Protect 3, AIA Enhanced Cancer Protect, AIA HealthShield Gold Max and more. On the right is what a search or AI crawler sees in the raw page before the browser builds it: the product list never loads and shows the words "Not initialised." in its place. The products a buyer can see simply are not there for a crawler to find.

Why it matters

When a crawler cannot discover a link, the product page behind it receives far less SEO value, and in some cases is not found at all. This single issue helps explain why only 15 AIA product pages win non branded traffic while Great Eastern has 195. The products exist, but search engines struggle to reach them.

Recommendation

Make sure every product in a hub is discoverable through a server rendered link that does not require JavaScript to appear.

3.2 · No canonical tags on about half the site
TechnicalWidespread
What we found

Of the 701 live pages we crawled, 375 (about 53 percent) carry no canonical tag, including 73 of 158 product pages.

Why it matters

A canonical tag is a simple instruction that tells search engines and AI platforms which version of a URL is the official one. Without it, the site is exposed to duplicate content confusion as it grows, and crawlers are left to guess.

Recommendation

Add a self referencing canonical tag to every page, pointing each URL to itself.

3.3 · Unnecessary hreflang on product pages wastes crawl budget
TechnicalCrawl budget
What we found

The travel product page below carries an hreflang tag pointing at an internal content path on a different host, rather than a real alternate language version.

  • https://www.aia.com.sg/en/our-products/travel-and-lifestyle/travel/aia-around-the-world-plus-ii
Why it matters

Crawl budget is the limited attention a search engine gives a site on each visit. Pointing it at internal duplicate paths means important pages get crawled less often.

Recommendation

Remove the hreflang tags from the product pages.

3.4 · No Open Graph or Twitter tags
TechnicalAEO
What we found

Pages do not carry Open Graph or Twitter tags.

Why it matters

These are the small pieces of code that tell platforms, and increasingly AI systems, what a page is about before they read the whole thing. They give both social platforms and AI crawlers a cleaner, faster understanding of each page.

Recommendation

Implement Open Graph and Twitter tags across the site.

3.5 · Multiple Heading 1 tags, and missing Heading 1 on many pages
TechnicalStructure
What we found

Several product pages and news release pages use more than one Heading 1. Our crawl also found 93 pages with no detectable Heading 1 at all, which points back to the rendering issue in 3.1.

  • https://www.aia.com.sg/en/our-products/health/critical-illness/aia-protect-3
  • https://www.aia.com.sg/en/about-aia/media-centre/press-releases/2026/aia-partners-insead
Why it matters

A page should have one clear Heading 1 so crawlers know the single main topic. Multiple Heading 1 tags dilute that signal, and a missing one removes it entirely.

Recommendation

Review heading structure and standardize to one clear Heading 1 per page.

3.6 · Missing structured data on content and product pages
TechnicalAEO
What we found

Blog and press release pages carry no Article or NewsArticle structured data. Beyond articles, the site is also missing FAQ, Service, and Breadcrumb structured data on the pages where each would apply. The homepage organization markup is in good shape, so this is about extending structured data to the page types that do not have it yet.

  • https://www.aia.com.sg/en/about-aia/sustainability/health-and-wellness/hyrox
  • https://www.aia.com.sg/en/about-aia/media-centre/press-releases/2026/aia-partners-insead
Why it matters

Structured data is the markup that helps AI systems understand what a page is, when it was published, and how it should be read. It is one of the most direct ways to make a page citable by AI.

Recommendation

Add Article and NewsArticle structured data to content and press releases, and add FAQ, Service, and Breadcrumb markup to the relevant pages.

3.7 · Press releases appear to be set to no index
TechnicalConfirm with team
What we found

The INSEAD partnership release appears to be marked so that search engines are told not to index it.

  • https://www.aia.com.sg/en/about-aia/media-centre/press-releases/2026/aia-partners-insead
Why it matters

No index means the page is hidden from search and from AI systems. If that is intentional, no action is needed. If it is not, AIA is hiding its own newsworthy moments from the platforms that would otherwise pick them up.

Recommendation

Confirm with the team whether this is intended. If not, set the press release pages to index.

3.8 · Redirects to a test environment and to error pages
TechnicalCrawl hygiene
What we found

One careers page redirects to a UAT testing environment on a wwwuat host rather than to a live page. Two URLs, the 2026 press releases landing path and the events path, redirect straight to a 404 error page. A couple of other pages use temporary redirects where a permanent one would be correct.

Why it matters

Redirects to a test environment should never happen on a live site, and redirects to a 404 waste crawl budget and frustrate anyone who lands on them. Together they signal a redirect setup that needs cleaning up.

Recommendation

Repair the redirect targets so they point to live, relevant pages, and switch permanent moves to permanent redirects.

3.9 · Slow mobile page speed and a missing favicon
TechnicalPerformance
What we found

A Lighthouse test of the homepage on mobile returned a performance score of 31 out of 100, with the largest content element taking far longer than the recommended two and a half seconds to appear and the main thread blocked for well over a second. The server itself responds quickly, so the problem is the weight of the page rather than slow hosting. The favicon, the small site icon browsers and search results show, was also not detected.

Why it matters

Slow, heavy pages get crawled less often and rank less well. The favicon is a small but visible trust and brand signal in search results and browser tabs.

Recommendation

Reduce the weight of the homepage and key templates, and add a favicon. Confirm the field data once Search Console is connected.

// 04 · Content Findings

Pages that do not yet speak to buyers or to AI

The product pages describe plans well, but they rarely frame who the plan is for, and AIA publishes very little educational content where most new buyers start.

4.1 · Product pages do not say who the product is for
ContentAEO
What we found

The critical illness page describes the plan, but it never frames the audience or the life situation the plan suits. Competitors do this well. Great Eastern and Manulife both spell out who their products are for on the page itself.

  • https://www.aia.com.sg/en/our-products/health/critical-illness/aia-protect-3
  • https://www.greateasternlife.com/sg/en/personal-insurance/our-products/health-insurance/great-supremehealth-main.html
  • https://www.manulife.com.sg/en/solutions/health/critical-illness/manulife-early-completecare.html
Visual evidence

How competitors do it. Great Eastern frames the same product around life stages, "for those starting a career", "for parents with young children", and "for foreigners residing in Singapore". Manulife adds a plain "Who is it for" section that names the audience directly. Both make it obvious to a reader, and to an AI, exactly which person each plan suits.

Why it matters

When someone asks an AI assistant "I just graduated and started my first job, what insurance should I get," the assistant looks for pages that clearly connect a product to a person and a moment. Without that context, AIA loses the discovery opportunity even when its product is the right one.

Recommendation

Add clear audience and life situation context to every product detail page, so both search engines and AI can match the page to real questions.

4.2 · Titles and meta descriptions do not match buyer intent
ContentOn page
What we found

The title on the enhanced cancer protection page reads "AIA Enhanced Cancer Protect | Critical Illness Insurance | AIA Singapore," which leads with the internal product name rather than the benefit a buyer searches for. A title closer to "Critical Illness Insurance for Cancer Protection: 100% Coverage | AIA Singapore" would speak to intent. This compounds with a wider gap: 292 pages, about 42 percent of the site, have no meta description at all, including 56 product pages.

  • https://www.aia.com.sg/en/our-products/health/critical-illness/aia-enhanced-cancer-protect
Why it matters

The right title and description help search engines and AI understand the product and make it easier to discover. A missing description leaves the platform to guess what to show.

Recommendation

Revisit titles, meta descriptions, and headings on the product pages together, in consultation with the internal team, since product naming often involves compliance.

4.3 · Almost no educational content for buyers
ContentBiggest opportunity
What we found

AIA publishes very little content that teaches a buyer how to choose insurance. Its single best non branded asset is one educational guide, the "5 types of insurance you should get in Singapore" article, which ranks on the first page for "life insurance," a term searched around 112,000 times a month, and is the most cited AIA page in AI answers today.

Why it matters

In the article space, roughly 90 percent of readers are new visitors who do not yet know which brand they want. That makes articles one of the most effective ways to bring in high intent, relevant audiences and turn them into leads. One good article is already doing the work of dozens of product pages.

Recommendation

Build an educational article engine that targets high volume non branded questions, modeled on the success of the existing guide.

The AI visibility work runs alongside everything, because each fix and each new page is also a step toward being the cited source rather than the mentioned brand.

// 06 · What to Prioritise

Sequencing the work by commercial impact

The data and the findings point to one clear theme. AIA has the authority but not the crawlable, well written pages to use it. We recommend tackling the highest impact work first.

  1. Make product pages discoverable. Fix the hub page links so every product is reachable through a server rendered link (3.1). This is the root cause of the 15 versus 195 product page gap.
  2. Repair the technical foundation. Add self referencing canonicals, remove the product hreflang, fix the UAT and 404 redirects, and standardize Heading 1 (3.2, 3.3, 3.5, 3.8). Largely labelling and cleanup work on pages that already exist.
  3. Rewrite product pages for buyers and AI. Add audience context, fix titles and the 292 missing meta descriptions, and add Service, FAQ, and Breadcrumb structured data (4.1, 4.2, 3.6).
  4. Build the educational article engine. Scale up content modelled on the existing guide that already ranks for "life insurance," to win non branded demand and feed authority to the product pages (4.3).
  5. Turn AI mentions into AI citations. For every question where a portal is cited instead of AIA, publish the equivalent AIA owned page, and improve homepage and template speed (Section 2, 3.9).
