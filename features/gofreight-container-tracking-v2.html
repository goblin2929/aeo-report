Frequently Asked Questions

What is container tracking software?
Container tracking software provides real-time visibility into the location and status of shipping containers throughout their journey. It automatically pulls data from ocean carriers to show container status—vessel departure, transshipment, port arrival, customs clearance, and delivery—without manual check-ins. For freight forwarders, this means proactive alerts, reduced demurrage, and better customer communication.
How does GoFreight track containers in real-time?
GoFreight connects directly to 125+ ocean carriers via API integrations, pulling container status updates automatically. When a container reaches a milestone—vessel departure, port arrival, customs hold, or delivery—GoFreight updates the shipment record, triggers automated alerts, and notifies both your team and customers through the portal.
Which carriers does GoFreight integrate with?
GoFreight integrates with 125+ carriers across ocean, air, and ground. These direct integrations provide real time tracking data without manual status checks.
Can my customers track their own shipments?
Yes. GoFreight includes a branded customer portal where your customers can track their shipments in real-time, view documents, and receive automated status updates—without calling your operations team. This self-service visibility reduces inbound inquiries and improves customer satisfaction.
How does container tracking reduce demurrage costs?
GoFreight sends proactive alerts when containers approach free time expiration, allowing your team to arrange pickup before demurrage fees hit. One GoFreight customer, United Cargo Management, reduced demurrage costs by 53% after implementing automated tracking alerts. The system also identifies potential delays early, giving you time to adjust plans.
