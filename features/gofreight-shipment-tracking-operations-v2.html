Frequently Asked Questions

What is shipment tracking software?
Shipment tracking software provides real-time visibility into shipment location and status across carriers and modes. For freight forwarders, this means one dashboard to see all shipments—ocean, air, and rail—instead of checking multiple carrier websites.
Can I track both ocean and air shipments?
Yes. GoFreight tracks both ocean and air shipments in a single dashboard. You can see container status for ocean freight and flight/AWB status for air freight, all with automated updates and alerts.
How does GoFreight integrate with carriers?
GoFreight integrates with 125+ carriers across ocean, air, and ground. Tracking data flows automatically into your dashboard without manual status checks.
Can my customers see tracking information?
Yes. GoFreight includes a branded customer portal where your shippers can track their own shipments, view documents, and receive automated status notifications—all without calling your team.
What is cargo management software?
Cargo management software provides freight forwarders with tools to manage the complete shipment lifecycle—from initial booking through documentation, tracking, and empty return. It handles both ocean and air cargo, including consolidation workflows.
Does GoFreight handle both ocean and air cargo?
Yes. GoFreight manages both ocean cargo (FCL, LCL, consolidation) and air cargo (HAWB/MAWB, flights) in one platform. You can track shipments across modes from a single dashboard.
Can I manage LCL consolidations?
Yes. GoFreight supports LCL consolidation workflows, allowing you to group multiple shippers into a single container. The system handles house bills, master bills, and split invoicing.
How do automated tracking alerts work?
GoFreight sends automated notifications for key shipment milestones—vessel arrivals, customs clearance, delays, and exceptions. You can configure alerts by event type and route them to your team or directly to customers through the portal.
