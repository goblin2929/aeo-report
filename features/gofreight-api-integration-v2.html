Frequently Asked Questions

What systems does GoFreight integrate with?
GoFreight integrates with 125+ ocean, air, and trucking carriers for real-time tracking; QuickBooks Online for two-way accounting sync; U.S. Customs (CBP/ACE) for ISF, AES, and AMS filing; and overseas agent networks via EDI for settlement and document exchange. An open REST API and webhooks allow custom connections to ERPs, data warehouses, BI tools, and any other system your operation requires.
How long does integration setup take?
GoFreight implementation typically takes 4-8 weeks, compared to 6-12 months for legacy freight systems. A dedicated integration support team and assigned Customer Success Manager guide you through the entire process—from data migration to go-live and beyond.
Can GoFreight connect to my accounting software?
Yes. GoFreight provides native two-way sync with both QuickBooks Online and QuickBooks Desktop. Invoices, payments, and financial data flow automatically between systems—no duplicate entry, no manual reconciliation. Your books stay current without extra work.
What if I need a custom integration?
GoFreight offers a full REST API with documentation at developers.gofreight.com. Webhook notifications provide real-time event triggers, so you can connect GoFreight to ERPs, data warehouses like Snowflake, BI tools, or build custom client-facing applications. EDI support is also available for partners who require legacy connectivity.
Does GoFreight support EDI for overseas agents?
Yes. GoFreight supports EDI connectivity for sharing shipment data, documents, and settlement information with your overseas agent network. This enables automated data exchange across your global operations without requiring agents to use the same system.
Will my data migrate from my current system?
Yes. GoFreight's onboarding team migrates your customer and partner records, rate agreements, and open shipments from your current system. Your team is productive within days of go-live, not months. A dedicated Customer Success Manager oversees the entire migration process.
