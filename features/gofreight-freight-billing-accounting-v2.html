Frequently Asked Questions

What is freight billing software?
Freight billing software handles the unique invoicing and financial needs of freight forwarding: generating invoices from shipment data, tracking profitability by job, managing agent settlements, and supporting multi-currency transactions. Unlike generic billing tools, it's built for how forwarders actually quote, invoice, and collect payment.
What accounting systems does GoFreight connect to?
GoFreight has an open API that connects to your existing accounting system, whether that's QuickBooks, Sage, NetSuite, or an enterprise ERP. QuickBooks users get a native connector with two-way sync. For other systems, GoFreight's open API supports custom integration with your accounting stack. GoFreight also includes built-in freight-specific accounting, so many customers run it standalone without needing an external tool at all.
Can I see profitability by shipment?
Yes. GoFreight shows profit and loss on every job—revenue, costs, and margin visible at the shipment level. You can see which lanes, customers, and services are most profitable.
How do agent settlements work in GoFreight?
GoFreight tracks amounts owed to and from your overseas partners at the shipment level. You can run settlement reports to see net positions, generate settlement statements, and record payments when settlements clear.
Can I match payments to invoices automatically?
Yes. GoFreight supports payment matching to apply incoming customer payments to outstanding invoices. You can match single payments to multiple invoices or partial payments to individual invoices.
How does multi-currency billing and payment work?
GoFreight handles multi-currency transactions end to end. Invoice customers in their preferred currency, receive payments in another, track costs in carrier currencies, and automatically manage exchange rate conversions and gains/losses for reporting.
What is freight settlement software?
Freight settlement software manages the complex financial flows in freight forwarding—customer receivables, agent settlements with overseas partners, and vendor payables to carriers and truckers. It provides visibility into what's owed in every direction.
Does GoFreight handle both accounts receivable and accounts payable?
Yes. GoFreight provides full AR and AP management—customer invoicing and payment tracking on the receivable side, vendor and carrier bill management on the payable side, with aging reports, credit limits, and collections tracking across both.
