What the experiment tells us:
  • Full rewrites of thin, low-CTR pages win biggest. Batch 3 delivered +230% clicks and a 5.4-position ranking jump (19.3 → 13.9). Rebuilding empty or thin bodies into structured content is the single highest-ROI refresh move.
  • CTR improved in every batch. The consistent levers were the Key Takeaways box and the FAQ rebuilt from real GSC queries. Even the light-touch Batch 2, with title, meta, and body preserved, nearly doubled CTR (0.14% → 0.26%) and added +19% clicks from those two changes alone.
  • Refresh beats net-new on effort: 40 existing articles produced +255 net clicks (+49%) in roughly two weeks.
  • Watch, Batch 2 impressions −33%: impressions dipped on a few high-volume informational pages while clicks and CTR rose, trading low-quality impressions for better-qualified clicks. We will keep watching to confirm it holds.

How AI citation counts work (methodology & validation). A "response" is one engine's answer to one tracked prompt on one day — WorkDuo re-asks every prompt on ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI roughly daily, so 28 active prompts produced 2,352 responses in April and 2,404 in May (~flat sampling; May simply has one more day). A "citation" is each time a GoFreight page appears in a response's source list; "pages cited" counts distinct pages. The May citation increase (4,379 → 4,937, +12.7%) is not an artifact of expanded prompt tracking: the 11 prompts added to the project were created June 5 and contributed zero April/May data, so the comparison runs on an identical 28-prompt panel. With sampling flat, citations per response rose 1.86 → 2.05 (+10.3%) — the engines are citing GoFreight more per answer, concentrated in specific pages (/product/integrations 10 → 244; cargowise-vs-gofreight 247 → 305). All citation figures were independently recounted from the WorkDuo API (both months reproduced exactly) and homepage URL variants are deduplicated.
