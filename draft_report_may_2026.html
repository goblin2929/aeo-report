Executive Summary

Month in Review

May 2026 was another steady growth month for GoFreight's organic search visibility. Total clicks rose 13.1% to 3,611 (up from 3,193 in April), while impressions climbed 17.0% to 1,275,200. The standout signal this month was off Google: AI platform sessions in GA more than doubled. Key drivers:

  • Non-branded content drove all of the month's net growth (+482 non-branded clicks), led by the blog folder at +25.8% (1,476 to 1,857 clicks)
  • The customs clearance guide kept compounding (179 to 297 clicks) and the new freight forwarding training courses article scaled from 5 to 174 clicks
  • AI platform sessions surged +103.1% (228 to 463 in GA) and Workduo AI visibility reached 24% (+2.2pp), with share of voice at 19.9% (+1.3pp)
  • Product and Solutions pages began surfacing: Product impressions up +577% (1k to 7k) and Solutions impressions up +202% (4.2k to 12.9k)

Blog Refresh Experiment

In May we ran a controlled content-refresh experiment across 40 existing blog articles, split into three batches with different selection logic and refresh depth, to learn which refresh style returns the most. Refresh dates: May 14 (Batch 1 & 2) and May 18 (Batch 3). Before vs after measured on Google Search Console.

Method by Batch

Approach Batch 1 Batch 2 Batch 3
Selection metric Top 15 by impressions Top 10 by clicks (Mar+Apr) High impressions + low CTR
Articles 15 10 15

Title / Meta changed Yes No (preserved) Yes

Key Takeaways box + FAQ rebuilt from GSC Yes Yes (the main visible add) Yes

Results (Before → After)

Batch Articles Clicks Impressions CTR Avg Position
Batch 1 (full rewrite) 15 78 → 109 (+40%) 40,887 → 40,419 (-1%) 0.19% → 0.27% 10.3 → 10.8
Batch 2 (light touch) 10 374 → 446 (+19%) 257,942 → 173,512 (-33%) 0.14% → 0.26% 8.9 → 9.4
Batch 3 (full rewrite, thin pages) 15 66 → 218 (+230%) 71,544 → 87,182 (+22%) 0.09% → 0.25% 19.3 → 13.9 (+5.4 ranks)
Combined (40 articles) 40 518 → 773 (+49%) 370,373 → 301,113 0.14% → 0.26% n/a

What the Experiment Tells Us

  • Full rewrites of thin, low-CTR pages win biggest. Batch 3 delivered +230% clicks and a 5.4-position ranking jump (19.3 to 13.9). Rebuilding empty or thin bodies into proper, structured content is the single highest-ROI refresh move.
  • CTR improved in every batch. The consistent levers were the Key Takeaways box and the FAQ rebuilt from real GSC queries. Even the light-touch Batch 2, which preserved title, meta, and body, nearly doubled CTR (0.14% to 0.26%) and added +19% clicks from those two changes alone.
  • Refresh beats rewrite-from-scratch on effort. 40 existing articles produced +255 net clicks (+49%) in roughly two weeks, faster and cheaper than net-new content.
  • Note on Batch 2 impressions (-33%): impressions dipped on a few high-volume informational pages while clicks and CTR rose, so the refresh traded low-quality impressions for better-qualified clicks. We will keep watching these to confirm it holds.

AI-Citation Publisher Outreach (Batch 2)

Why This Matters for AEO

When AI platforms answer high-intent queries like "Best Freight Management Software for Enterprise," they pull from and cite third-party listicles and software-directory pages, not just vendor websites. If GoFreight is named inside those cited sources, its chance of being mentioned in the AI answer rises sharply. So we are reaching out to the publishers that AI engines already cite for freight management, TMS, customs brokerage, and NVOCC software, to get GoFreight added to their lists. This is the off-page half of the AEO program, complementing the on-page work (llm.txt, schema, author credentials).

We contacted 11 cited publishers and collected their placement terms. Pricing models vary from free profile claims to reciprocal link exchanges to paid positional placements. Most are now awaiting a go / no-go decision from the GoFreight team.
